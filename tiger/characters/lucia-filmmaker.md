## Who they are
Italian-born documentary director, ten years in, on an expiring O-1 with a new film in post and a US distributor waiting. Her record is unmistakably arts: a Sundance official selection that also screened at IDFA and Hot Docs, an NYT Critic's Pick, a Variety review, a theatrical/streaming distribution deal, voting membership in the DGA and IDA, and a lead creative role on every film she's directed. Warm but exacting, story-first — "that's not how this field is judged." Burned before by an O-1A-shaped intake that asked her for "scholarly articles" and "patents" and drew an RFE.

## Jobs to be done (what they hire the MODEL OUTPUT for)
- Confirm she's screened as **O-1B arts** against the right six criteria — and catch instantly if she's been handed the O-1A eight in disguise — **[[qualify-screening]]**.
- A petition that argues distinction the way a real arts-O-1 does, mapping selections/reviews/deal/guild to lead role / recognition / reviews & press / commercial-critical success — **[[draft]]**, regenerable per section — **[[draft-section]]**.

## Senior-quality bar
At least as good as an arts-specialist immigration associate who knows a documentary director is judged on lead creative role, critical reviews, festival recognition, and commercial/critical success — and would never ask a filmmaker for a patent. A senior rejects any draft built on the O-1A eight, any mis-bucketed review, or any invented festival/review/deal.

## Time-saved (motivation)
LLM-less way = $8k–$12k + 2–3 months of an arts-O-1 firm, plus RFE risk if the packet argues the wrong criteria. Wins if it screens her correctly as O-1B and drafts the distinction argument in an afternoon; if it scores her against the O-1A eight it's not faster — it's the exact RFE trap she's already lived.
