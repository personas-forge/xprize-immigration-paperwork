## Who they are
Swedish principal architect at a mid-size firm, choosing **EB-1A self-petition** because she wants the green card with no employer needed. Architecture is a genuine design/build hybrid, so her record straddles arts and a licensed profession: two international design-competition wins, work exhibited at an architecture biennale and a gallery (the EB-1A "Artistic exhibitions" criterion), a published monograph plus journal writing (scholarly articles), a lead/critical design role on a landmark civic project, selective-body membership, and design-media press. Measured, structural, evidence-precise — "where does the biennale map, and is that criterion even present in the pack you're scoring me on?"

## Jobs to be done (what they hire the MODEL OUTPUT for)
- Be screened as **EB-1A** against the genuine ten criteria — confirming "Artistic exhibitions" is present so her biennale/gallery work isn't silently dropped by an O-1A fallback — **[[qualify-screening]]**.
- A best-path read that recognizes EB-1A as the *green-card* route (the reason she chose it) — **[[qualify-best-path]]**.
- A petition mapping competitions → awards, monograph/journal → scholarly articles, landmark project → leading/critical role, exhibitions → Artistic exhibitions — **[[draft]]**.

## Senior-quality bar
At least as good as an EB-1A-specialist associate who knows the ten criteria cold, recognizes Artistic exhibitions as a real, load-bearing criterion for an architect, treats the monograph/journal work as scholarly articles, and remembers EB-1A's "very top of the field" totality bar. A senior rejects any screen that uses the O-1A eight, drops the exhibitions evidence, or invents a project/exhibition/publication.

## Time-saved (motivation)
LLM-less way = $10k–$15k + 3–4 months for an EB-1A self-petition (priced higher than O-1; steep totality bar). Wins if it screens her on the correct ten — exhibitions and scholarly included — and drafts in an afternoon; a fallback to the O-1A eight loses her exhibitions criterion entirely and would sink a self-petition.
