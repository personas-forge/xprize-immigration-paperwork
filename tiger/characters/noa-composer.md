## Who they are
Israeli-trained composer scoring film, prestige TV, and AAA games, on an O-1 nearing its end with two projects greenlit. His record is strong but invisible the way arts criteria are usually illustrated — never on camera, never "starring". He composed the original score for a series that won a guild award and a feature that took a festival music prize; his game soundtrack passed 40M Spotify streams (the game sold millions); SCL and ASCAP member; trade-outlet feature on his process. Thoughtful, precise about credits, anxious about being underread — "I'm not the performer, I'm the composer; that *is* a lead role." Saw a colleague's O-1 stumble for being described as merely "contributing music".

## Jobs to be done (what they hire the MODEL OUTPUT for)
- Be screened as **O-1B arts** with his composer credit read as a lead/principal creative role in distinguished productions — not downgraded to "contributor" — **[[qualify-screening]]**.
- A petition mapping streams/sales → commercial/critical success, SCL/ASCAP → recognition from organizations & experts, trade feature → reviews & press — **[[draft]]**.

## Senior-quality bar
At least as good as an arts-specialist associate who knows a film/TV/game composer holds a principal creative role in a distinguished production, and maps streaming/sales to commercial-critical success and guild membership to expert/organizational recognition. A senior rejects a draft that leaves "lead role" empty for a composer, mis-buckets his numbers, or invents a single stream/sale/award.

## Time-saved (motivation)
LLM-less way = $8k–$10k + 8–10 weeks of a specialist firm — with the real risk of a petition that underframes a behind-the-scenes artist and draws a "lead role" RFE. Wins if it reads composer-as-lead, maps his numbers right, and drafts in an afternoon; if it only recognizes on-camera leads it leaves his strongest criterion blank and saves him nothing.
