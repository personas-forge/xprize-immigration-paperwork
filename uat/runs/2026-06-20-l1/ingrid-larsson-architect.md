## The one test that matters: am I scored on the genuine EB-1A TEN — exhibitions AND scholarly both present — or silently dropped to the O-1A eight?

I built the surface model by following the import chain from each entry affordance to the code behind it. The headline: **the O-1A-fallback trap the protocol warned me about cannot bite me, and my two load-bearing criteria both exist.**

`packFor()` falls back to O-1A only for an *unknown/unset* classification (`packs.ts:222-225`). EB-1A is a **live US program** (`jurisdictions.ts:63`), so the fallback never fires on my path:

- The EB-1A pack is the genuine **ten** (`packs.ts:142-168`): Awards, Membership, Press, Judging, Original contribution, **Scholarly articles** (`:152`), **Artistic exhibitions** (`:153-158`), Leading or critical role, High remuneration, Commercial success in the arts. **Both "Artistic exhibitions" AND "Scholarly articles" are present** — the EB-1A-defining pairing. The O-1A eight has *no* Artistic-exhibitions criterion (`packs.ts:90-98`); my pack is not O-1A relabeled.
- `parseQualifyRequest` keeps `"EB-1A"` because `isLiveProgram("EB-1A")` is true (`qualification.ts:108-111`, `jurisdictions.ts:101-103`).
- `buildQualifyPrompt` enumerates `packFor("EB-1A").criteria` **by name** — the ten — and tells the model "use these exact names" (`qualification.ts:120-141`).
- `CriteriaReport` renders `result.criteria` straight from the pack with **no hardcoded O-1A list** (`CriteriaReport.tsx:119-153`).
- The qualify route **persists `req.classification` + the scored criteria** onto a new case (`api/qualify/route.ts:96-101`), so the case is born EB-1A.
- On `/dashboard/cases/[id]` the **DB classification** flows into the criteria header (`§ II — EB-1A criteria`, `CaseDetailView.tsx:143`), the **EvidenceVault buckets** (`CaseDetailView.tsx:193-196` → `criteriaNames(classification)`, `EvidenceVault.tsx:55`), the **DraftStudio** (`CaseDetailView.tsx:202-205`), and the roadmap. The page reads `stored.classification` from the persisted case — never a default (`page.tsx:76`).
- EB-1A carries a **`verified` validation record**: 8 CFR 204.5(h)(3), "3 of 10 criteria", note "*The ten criteria in the pack match 8 CFR 204.5(h)(3)(i)-(x) verbatim in set and order*" (`validation.ts:103-126`), surfaced on the screen (`QualifyPanel.tsx:159-167`).

**Acceptance criterion 1: PASS in code.** My screen is the EB-1A ten with Artistic exhibitions and Scholarly articles both on it. **Criterion 4: PASS in code** — EB-1A is the only program in `GREEN_CARD_PROGRAMS` (`best-path.ts:29`), and `rationaleFor` appends "*It is also a green card (permanent residence)*" (`best-path.ts:120-121`); the comparison card tags it "green card" (`BestPathFinder.tsx:241`). ## Journey 2 — draft-petition-letter · **L1-conditional**

**Walkthrough:** From my EB-1A case the draft is generated **one argument section per qualifying criterion, by name** (`drafting.ts:174-208`, prompt uses `${req.classification}` throughout). So if "Artistic exhibitions" is scored Met/Strong it earns a section headed "Artistic exhibitions", and "Scholarly articles" its own — the EB-1A grammar, correct. On the DB path the draft route reloads my **persisted EB-1A criteria** (name/status/evidence/rationale via `criterionLine`) plus the petitioner + classification from the case gate, and **fuses my vault's real exhibit facts** (`draftOperation.ts:147-161`, `attachExhibits`). Citation discipline forbids inventing awards/exhibitions/publications and forbids case-law (`drafting.ts:181-192`), and `auditCitations` quarantines any `(Exhibit N)` that doesn't resolve to a real on-file document (`drafting.ts:593-608`, surfaced in DraftStudio). A fabricated competition or invented biennale year has **no grounding path** — a genuine strength against my "fabricated exhibition" peeve (`il-draft-01`, strength). The G1.1 continuity fix shipped: a single-section regenerate is fed the letter's other sections as read-only context, so it won't duplicate the intro or contradict siblings (`drafting.ts:222-278`, `draftOperation.ts:178`).

Two caveats, both for L2. First, the draft prompt has **no EB-1A/arts-hybrid guidance** — nothing tells the model that "Artistic exhibitions" for an architect means a biennale/gallery showing of *work* (not a fine-arts gallery only), or that EB-1A also demands a "very top of the field" totality read (`il-draft-02`, major; the prompt argues purely from the criterion name + my persisted evidence). Whether my "Artistic exhibitions" and "Scholarly articles" sections read like a real EB-1A petition or generic filler rides on the model's defaults — invisible to static analysis. Second, the *raw monograph/CV text* is not re-fed to the draft prompt; it argues from the per-criterion paraphrases the qualify step captured — but that is **accepted PN-DRAFT-01/G1.3** (L2 proved the live draft already names the supplied specifics because the qualify model captures them into the persisted criteria), so it's a scope_note, not a new finding.

**Grounding score: 4/6** (persisted criteria ✓, evidence/rationale ✓, vault exhibit facts ✓, the rest of the letter on regenerate ✓; the raw CV is accepted-out, and there's no EB-1A-specific framing). **Est. time-saved-if-it-worked:** an afternoon to a red-line-ready draft my attorney signs — the headline promise, contingent on the EB-1A prose landing.

## First-person review — Ingrid's felt verdict

I came in braced for the one betrayal an architect cannot forgive: being quietly scored on the O-1A eight, where the **Artistic exhibitions** criterion *doesn't exist*, and watching my Venice Biennale and gallery showings fall on the floor. The code does not do that. My pack is the genuine ten, exhibitions and scholarly both sitting on it, EB-1A is correctly the green card, and — the part that earns my trust — there's a *validation record* that says, in writing, the ten match 8 CFR 204.5(h)(3)(i)-(x) verbatim in set and order. That is a system that respects the distinction I care about. The vault has a literal "Artistic exhibitions" bucket; my biennale has somewhere true to land; the draft can't invent a competition I didn't win. As a structure, it's *coherent*. I'd keep going.

What stops me short of "delighted" is the seam between the keyword preview and the real read. The free "find my best path" — the first thing the funnel hands me — reads "**Featured** at the Venice Architecture Biennale" as *press*, because the regex for Press fires before the one for Artistic exhibitions. For most users that's a harmless approximation. For *me* it's the precise misread that sinks a self-petition, shown on the very first screen, and I have to trust a soft-gate promise that "the full screening reads your whole record in depth" to undo it. The build is honest that the preview is a keyword read — I respect the labeling — but it means my exhibitions only get scored correctly *after* I commit and pay. And the deeper draft has no EB-1A-specific instinct in its prompt: whether my "Artistic exhibitions" section argues like an EB-1A specialist who knows a biennale is load-bearing, or like generic filler, is something I cannot see in the code — it's the whole reason L2 has to run the live model on my actual record. The "O1-" on my green-card file number is a small thing, but it's the kind of small thing that makes a precise person wonder what else was relabeled rather than rebuilt.

Would I adopt it? Yes — conditionally. The skeleton is right where it has to be, and right is rare. But I'd tell a peer: take the "I already know my visa → EB-1A" door, don't trust the keyword card on your exhibitions, and judge it on the *deep* read — that's where my biennale either becomes an Artistic-exhibitions argument a senior would sign, or doesn't.
