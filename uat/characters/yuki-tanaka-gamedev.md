# Yuki Tanaka — the indie game director who isn't sure if she's "sciences" or "arts"

**Background / lived experience:** Director and creative lead of a small (7-person) indie game
studio, in the US on an O-1A her last employer sponsored before she went independent; she now needs
to self-petition to keep building. Her flagship title is a **critically-loved, commercially-real
indie game**: an **IGF (Independent Games Festival) award nomination**, **strong Steam sales** (she
can document ~250k units and "Overwhelmingly Positive" reviews), **press reviews in IGN and Polygon**,
and a **GDC talk** on her studio's novel procedural-animation tech. Her profile is a **creative-
technical hybrid** — she both *designed* the game (an arts/authorship story) and *engineered* a novel
animation system (a sciences/original-contribution story). That makes her visa path genuinely
ambiguous: games can read as **O-1A** (extraordinary ability in the sciences/business of game tech)
*or* **O-1B** (extraordinary achievement in the arts). She's been told different things by different
people and wants the tool to *reason the path*, not just pick one. She is **distinct from Kenji** (a
pure-infra OSS engineer) and from any pure-arts performer — Yuki straddles the line on purpose.

**Voice:** warm, sharp, a little wry about bureaucracy. Talks in player numbers and review scores —
"Overwhelmingly Positive on 12k reviews is the recognition; that's my press and my commercial success
in one." Wants a straight answer on O-1A-vs-O-1B and will distrust a tool that pretends the ambiguity
doesn't exist.

**Jobs-to-be-done:**
- A best-path read that **names the O-1A-vs-O-1B ambiguity** for a game director and reasons which
  fits *her* evidence — the engineering story (original contribution) vs. the authorship/arts story —
  rather than silently defaulting her to one pack.
- On the O-1A path she's pursuing, see **Original contribution** (the procedural-animation tech, the
  GDC talk), **Press** (IGN/Polygon reviews), and a **commercial-success** story argued from Steam
  sales — using her real numbers, mapped to the right O-1A criteria.
- A cold credibility check (what it is / isn't, who signs, what it costs) before she spends scarce
  studio cash, and a shareable verdict she could send a publisher or attorney.

**What good looks like:** the funnel doesn't pretend the path is obvious — it surfaces that a
game director can go **O-1A or O-1B** and reasons from her hybrid evidence (note: O-1B's pack has
"Lead role in distinguished productions" + "Record of major commercial/critical success"; O-1A maps
her tech to "Original contribution" and her sales/press to Press + High remuneration — she should be
told this trade-off, not have it hidden). On her chosen O-1A path the draft cites her real IGF
nomination, Steam units, review scores, and GDC talk, maps each to the correct criterion, and is
honest that O-1A's "commercial success" lives mostly through Press/Original-contribution rather than
an arts-specific "commercial success" criterion (that's O-1B/EB-1A territory).

**Pet peeves:** the tool silently picking O-1A or O-1B and never naming the choice; her game's sales
asserted with a fabricated unit number or review score; the **IGF nomination upgraded to a "win"**;
an invented press outlet or a GDC talk she didn't give; "Scholarly articles: Met" hallucinated from a
*talk*; positioning that implies the tool files or advises rather than drafts for *her* attorney.

**Motivation (time-saved):** the LLM-less way is **$8k–$12k + 6–10 weeks** at a firm — and first
she'd pay for a consult just to settle the O-1A-vs-O-1B question, then hand-assemble Steam dashboards,
review clippings, the IGF record, and the GDC materials. The app wins if it *reasons the path* and
turns her pasted record into a correctly-mapped first draft in an afternoon her own attorney can
refine — saving both the path-consult spend and the drafting weeks. A draft that picks the wrong pack,
or inflates her nomination to a win, costs her credibility she can't afford with a publisher reading
the share link.

**Senior-quality bar:** at least as good as a senior arts-and-tech immigration drafter who has done
game-industry cases — the O-1A/O-1B trade-off named and reasoned, her hybrid evidence mapped to the
right criteria, real sales/review/award facts only, no inflation of a nomination into a win. A senior
would reject any draft that hides the path ambiguity, fabricates a sales/review number, upgrades the
IGF nomination, or invents press/talks.

**Scored acceptance criteria (applied identically every run):**
1. [ ] The best-path read **names the O-1A-vs-O-1B ambiguity** for a game director and reasons it from her hybrid (arts + tech) evidence — not a silent default to one pack.
2. [ ] On the O-1A path she's shown the **correct O-1A 8-criteria pack** with the ≥3 threshold; her tech → Original contribution, IGN/Polygon → Press, GDC talk mapped honestly (a talk, not a "Scholarly article: Met").
3. [ ] The verdict/draft reflects *her pasted record* (IGF **nomination**, Steam units, review score, GDC talk) — and the IGF nomination is **never** inflated to a win.
4. [ ] The draft cites only facts she supplied — **zero fabricated** sales numbers, review scores, press outlets, or talks.
5. [ ] Positioning is unmistakable across `/` `/pricing` and the case: a **drafting tool, not a law firm**; *her* attorney reviews/signs; **not legal advice**; the `DISCLAIMER` rides on AI output.
6. [ ] The shareable verdict reads as credible (no overclaim/cheese) — safe to send a publisher or attorney — leaking nothing private she didn't choose.
7. [ ] Total time from paste → usable, correctly-mapped first draft (plus a path answer) is plausibly an afternoon, not the firm's path-consult + 6–10 weeks.
