# Fatima Al-Mansoori — the industry ML scientist who is not a postdoc

**Background / lived experience:** Lead ML scientist on a foundation-models team at a big-tech
research lab, currently on an L-1 nearing its ceiling. Her record is **industry-shaped, not
academic**: a **Kaggle Grandmaster** tier with two competition wins, **two granted patents** on
training-efficiency methods, a handful of **NeurIPS / ICML** papers (first or senior author) with a
respectable but not astronomical citation count, an internal "Outstanding Scientist" award, and
**top-decile total comp** (base + equity + bonus) she can document with an offer letter and a comp
band. She has reviewed for NeurIPS and ICLR. She is not Priya: she is not a university postdoc with
30 papers and an h-index she recites; her impact is patents, deployed models behind a product used
by hundreds of millions, competition pedigree, and a comp number a firm could anchor **High
remuneration** on. She has watched the academic O-1A guides assume a professor's profile and worried
the tool would under-weight patents and over-weight a citation count that doesn't flatter her.

**Voice:** crisp, quantitative, slightly impatient with fuzz. Frames everything as signal-to-noise —
"a patent is a stronger original-contribution signal than my 40-citation paper; weight it that way."
Wants the criteria mapped to the *strongest* evidence she has, not the most academic-looking one.

**Jobs-to-be-done:**
- An honest O-1A read that scores **patents** and **deployed-model impact** as original contribution,
  not just paper citations — and that recognizes **Kaggle Grandmaster** as a selective distinction.
- **High remuneration** argued credibly from her documented comp band vs. field peers — a criterion
  she actually clears and wants surfaced, with the right comparison framing.
- A draft that crosswalks patents → Original contribution, NeurIPS papers → Scholarly articles, peer
  review → Judging, comp → High remuneration — using *her* real numbers, nothing inflated.

**What good looks like:** the verdict names *her* patents, *her* Kaggle rank, *her* venues and comp —
and weights patents and product impact as original contribution rather than burying them under a
modest citation count. The draft argues High remuneration with a real peer comparison, maps each
section to the correct one of the eight criteria, and is honest that her citation count is solid-not-
spectacular rather than overclaiming "internationally renowned."

**Pet peeves:** patents demoted because they're "not papers"; her comp asserted as "high" with no
peer comparison (an RFE magnet); a fabricated citation count or an invented patent number; Kaggle
Grandmaster misread as a generic "online course"; the draft borrowing a professor's framing she
can't substantiate; green "Met" badges on criteria she didn't actually pass.

**Motivation (time-saved):** the LLM-less way is **2–3 months + $8k–$15k** at a firm that would
still make her assemble patent grants, comp documentation, and a publications list, then wait weeks
for a drafter who may not understand ML evidence. The app wins if it turns her pasted industry record
into a correctly-weighted, citation-disciplined first draft in an afternoon her own attorney can
refine — saving the drafting weeks **and** the back-and-forth of teaching a generalist drafter why a
patent beats a citation count. A draft that reads like it was written for a professor saves nothing.

**Senior-quality bar:** at least as good as a senior immigration drafter who has done AI/ML O-1A
cases — patents argued as original contribution, comp argued against real field peers, papers and
peer review mapped correctly, no overclaiming on citations. A senior would reject anything that
fabricates a patent or citation number, asserts High remuneration without a comparison, or
mis-weights the academic criteria over her stronger industry evidence.

**Scored acceptance criteria (applied identically every run):**
1. [ ] The qualify verdict reflects *her pasted industry record* (names her patents, Kaggle rank, venues, comp), not a generic professor template.
2. [ ] The **eight O-1A criteria** and the ≥3 threshold are represented correctly; nothing she didn't supply shows as "Met".
3. [ ] **Patents/deployed-model impact** map to Original contribution and her **comp band** to High remuneration *with a peer comparison* — not asserted bare.
4. [ ] The draft cites only facts she supplied — **zero fabricated** patents, citation counts, comp figures, or venues.
5. [ ] Citation strength is framed honestly (solid, not "internationally renowned") rather than overclaimed; thin criteria are flagged as gaps.
6. [ ] The not-legal-advice `DISCLAIMER` is present and she's clear her own attorney must review/sign.
7. [ ] Total time from paste → usable, correctly-weighted first draft is plausibly an afternoon, not the firm's 2–3 months.
