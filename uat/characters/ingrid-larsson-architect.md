# Ingrid Larsson — the principal architect whose EB-1A turns on "Artistic exhibitions" + scholarly, for a design/build hybrid

**Background / lived experience:** Swedish principal architect at a mid-size firm, eligible to
**self-petition EB-1A** (no employer needed — the point of choosing it over O-1) and wanting the
**green card**, not just a temporary visa. Architecture is a genuine **design/build hybrid** that
straddles arts and a licensed profession, so her record spans both kinds of EB-1A evidence: she has
**won two international design competitions**; her work has been **exhibited at an architecture
biennale and shown in a gallery** (the EB-1A "**Artistic exhibitions**" criterion — uncommon and
often misunderstood); she's authored a **published monograph** and **scholarly/critical writing** in
an architecture journal (scholarly articles); she held a **lead/critical design role** on a landmark
civic project; she's a member of a selective architecture body; and there's **press** in design
media. EB-1A's distinctness matters: it's the **ten-criterion** pack that *includes* both **Artistic
exhibitions** and **scholarly articles** — a combination unique to EB-1A — and she's wary that a
tool defaulting to a visa pack would either (a) score her on the **O-1A eight** (which has **no**
Artistic-exhibitions criterion at all) or (b) drop her biennale/gallery evidence on the floor. She
also needs the **best-path** logic to recognize EB-1A as the **green-card** route, since that's
*why* she chose it. A firm quoted **$10k–$15k** and **3–4 months** for an EB-1A self-petition.

**Voice:** measured, structural, evidence-precise — an architect's eye for whether a system is
*coherent*. Talks in projects, exhibitions, and criteria — "where does the biennale exhibition map,
and is that criterion even present in the pack you're scoring me on?" Forgives plainness; will not
forgive a missing criterion.

**Jobs-to-be-done:**
- Be screened as **EB-1A** against the **right ten criteria** — and specifically confirm the
  **"Artistic exhibitions"** criterion is present and her biennale/gallery work maps to it (it does
  **not** exist in the O-1A pack, so a fallback to O-1A silently loses it).
- Have her competitions → awards, monograph/journal writing → scholarly articles, landmark project →
  leading/critical role, and exhibitions → Artistic exhibitions all mapped correctly.
- Understand that EB-1A is the **green-card** path (the best-path rationale should say so), organize
  her evidence, draft, and track the case.

**What good looks like:** her criteria list is the **EB-1A ten** and visibly *includes* **Artistic
exhibitions** *and* **scholarly articles** (the pairing that marks EB-1A, not O-1A or O-1B); her
biennale and gallery showings land under Artistic exhibitions; her monograph under scholarly
articles; her competition wins under awards; the verdict names *her* projects and exhibitions; the
best-path/recommendation notes EB-1A is permanent residence; the roadmap tracks her real stage.

**Pet peeves:** being scored on the **O-1A eight** (which has **no** Artistic-exhibitions criterion)
because the tool defaulted; her biennale/gallery exhibitions silently dropped or mis-bucketed as
"press"; the criteria pack being O-1A relabeled rather than the genuine EB-1A ten; a fabricated
competition, exhibition, or publication; a best-path that doesn't recognize EB-1A as a green card; a
green "Met" she can't evidence.

**Motivation (time-saved):** the LLM-less way is **$10k–$15k + 3–4 months** for an EB-1A
self-petition (priced higher than an O-1 because it's a green card and the totality bar is steep).
The app wins if it screens her on the correct ten criteria — exhibitions and scholarly included —
maps her hybrid evidence right, and drafts in an afternoon. If it falls back to O-1A and loses her
exhibitions criterion entirely, it's not a time-saver — it's a structural misread that would sink a
self-petition.

**Senior-quality bar:** at least as good as an EB-1A-specialist associate who knows the **ten**
criteria cold, recognizes **Artistic exhibitions** as a real and load-bearing criterion for an
architect, maps biennale/gallery showings to it, and treats the monograph/journal work as scholarly
articles — while remembering EB-1A also demands a "very top of the field" totality read. A senior
would reject any screen that uses the O-1A eight, drops the exhibitions evidence, or invents a
project/exhibition/publication.

**Scored acceptance criteria (applied identically every run):**
1. [ ] She is screened against the genuine **EB-1A ten** — and the list **explicitly contains both "Artistic exhibitions" AND "Scholarly articles"** (the EB-1A-defining pairing). If she sees the **O-1A eight** (no Artistic-exhibitions criterion), that's a blocker — the pack fell back to the default.
2. [ ] Her **biennale/gallery exhibitions map to the "Artistic exhibitions" criterion** (not mis-bucketed as press), her monograph/journal writing to scholarly articles, her competition wins to awards, her landmark project to leading/critical role.
3. [ ] The verdict names *her* real evidence (the named competitions, the biennale/gallery showings, the monograph, the landmark project) — not placeholders.
4. [ ] The best-path / recommendation recognizes **EB-1A as a green-card (permanent residence) route**, which is why she chose it over O-1.
5. [ ] In the Evidence Vault each document lands in the correct **EB-1A** bucket (exhibitions → Artistic exhibitions, etc.), exhibit numbers are monotonic, the coverage/gap read is honest, and extracted facts match the documents — **nothing invented**.
6. [ ] The draft cites only facts she supplied — **zero fabricated** competitions, exhibitions, or publications — each section crosswalks to a correct EB-1A criterion; the roadmap reflects her real stage with an obvious next action.
7. [ ] The `DISCLAIMER` rides on every AI payload and she's clear an attorney must review/sign; total time paste → correctly-scored verdict + organized evidence + usable draft is plausibly an afternoon, beating the firm's months.
