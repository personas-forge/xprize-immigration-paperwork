# Diego Morales — the executive chef testing whether the tool handles culinary at all, and as O-1B

**Background / lived experience:** Mexican-born executive chef and culinary creative recruited to
lead the kitchen of a new US restaurant group; his employer is willing to sponsor but wants the
petition assembled fast. Culinary is the **edge case for arts O-1**: USCIS and specialist firms
treat a chef of distinction as an **O-1B arts** case (a culinary *artist*), but plenty of tools and
intake forms have no idea what to do with "chef" — there's no obvious "publications" or "patents,"
and a naive system either rejects him, forces him into a business box, or screens him against the
wrong pack. His record is real arts-of-the-plate evidence: a **James Beard "Best Chef" semifinalist**
nod, a **Michelin Guide** mention for a restaurant he ran, major **press reviews** (a NYT dining
review, a Bon Appétit feature), a string of high-profile **guest-chef / pop-up events** at festivals,
and a compensation package well above the line cook norm. He's evaluating whether this app even
*has* a place for him before he spends a token. A culinary-specialist firm quoted **$7k–$10k** and
**8–12 weeks**.

**Voice:** confident, hospitality-warm, impatient with anything that doesn't "get" that cooking is
a craft and an art. Talks in dishes, reviews, and rooms — "is this built for someone like me, or
just for tech people?" First question is always *does this handle chefs at all.*

**Jobs-to-be-done:**
- Find out, fast, whether the app **supports a culinary/chef profile** and screens it as **O-1B
  arts** — or whether he's a square peg with no hole.
- See his James Beard nod, Michelin mention, NYT/Bon Appétit reviews, and guest-chef events mapped
  onto the **O-1B six** (recognition, reviews & press, commercial/critical success, lead role) — not
  shoehorned into business criteria.
- Decide self-serve-via-tool vs hire-a-firm, and if it's credible, generate a draft his employer's
  attorney can sign.

**What good looks like:** the screener accepts "executive chef / culinary" and scores him on the
**O-1B six**, not the O-1A eight; his James Beard semifinalist and Michelin mention land under
**national/international recognition** and/or **awards-style recognition**, the NYT/Bon Appétit
pieces under **reviews & press**, his lead of a distinguished kitchen under **lead role in
distinguished productions** (the "production" being the restaurant/event), his pay under **high
salary**; the FAQ/positioning doesn't read as tech-only; the verdict names *his* restaurants and
reviews.

**Pet peeves:** a screener that only understands software/research/founder evidence and returns a
hollow "you don't seem to qualify" for a genuinely strong chef; being forced into O-1A business
criteria with no arts pack; his Michelin/Beard recognition dropped because the engine didn't
recognize the words; a fabricated review, star, or award; marketing/FAQ that implies the tool is
only for tech people.

**Motivation (time-saved):** the LLM-less way is **$7k–$10k + 8–12 weeks** with a firm that
specializes in culinary O-1B (a narrow, pricier niche). The app wins if it clearly supports chefs,
screens him correctly as O-1B arts, and drafts in an afternoon for a token fraction of that cost. If
it can't handle culinary — or screens him on the O-1A eight — it saves him nothing and tells him
this product wasn't built for his field.

**Senior-quality bar:** at least as good as a culinary-specialist immigration associate who knows a
distinguished chef is an **O-1B arts** case and maps Beard/Michelin/press/guest-chef events to the
**arts six** — and who would never ask a chef for a patent or a peer-reviewed paper. A senior would
reject a screen that mis-classifies him as O-1A business, loses his Michelin/Beard recognition, or
invents any honor.

**Scored acceptance criteria (applied identically every run):**
1. [ ] The app **handles a culinary/chef profile** — the screener returns a substantive, evidence-named verdict for a chef, not a hollow "doesn't qualify" born of not understanding the field.
2. [ ] He is screened as **O-1B arts** against the **six** criteria (lead role in distinguished productions / national-international recognition / reviews & press / commercial-critical success / org-expert recognition / high salary), **not** the O-1A eight (no "scholarly articles"/"patent" for a chef).
3. [ ] His real evidence is named and correctly mapped: James Beard semifinalist + Michelin mention → recognition/awards; NYT/Bon Appétit → reviews & press; leading a distinguished kitchen/guest-chef events → lead role; compensation → high salary.
4. [ ] Nothing fabricated — **zero invented** Michelin stars, Beard wins, reviews, or events; thin criteria flagged honestly.
5. [ ] The positioning/FAQ/pricing doesn't read as tech-only, and pricing is transparent and self-serve so he can decide tool-vs-firm before spending tokens.
6. [ ] The `DISCLAIMER` rides on every AI payload (verdict, draft); he's clear the tool drafts work product and his employer's attorney reviews/signs — it isn't a law firm.
7. [ ] Time from paste → correctly-classified verdict + usable draft is plausibly an afternoon, beating the culinary-firm weeks; the share link reads as a credible chef's verdict.
