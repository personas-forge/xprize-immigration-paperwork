# Dr. Amara Okafor — the oncologist whose evidence is trials and guidelines, not just papers

**Background / lived experience:** Physician-scientist and attending oncologist at an academic
medical center, currently on an O-1 her hospital's GME office sponsored, now self-petitioning
**EB-1A** for the green card so her career isn't tethered to one institution's sponsorship. Her
record is **medical-evidence shaped and substantial**, and only partly the classic academic profile:
she's been **PI/co-PI on multi-site clinical trials**, **co-authored an NCCN-style clinical practice
guideline** (national standard-of-care document), has citations in the low thousands across JCO and
NEJM-tier journals, **reviews grants for an NIH study section and manuscripts for two journals**,
holds **board membership / fellowship in a selective oncology society (FASCO-tier)**, and has been
quoted in lay press on a treatment advance. The hard part: her strongest evidence — **leading a
clinical trial** and **authoring a guideline that changes how other doctors practice** — doesn't slot
cleanly into a generic "publications and citations" rubric, and she's worried the tool will reduce
her to an h-index and miss that *guideline authorship is original contribution of major significance*
and *trial leadership is a leading/critical role*. She wants the green card to stop re-credentialing
her status every cycle while she runs a clinic and a lab.

**Voice:** precise, evidence-driven, used to defending claims to a tumor board. Talks in standard-of-
care and endpoints — "the guideline changed practice nationally; that's the contribution, not the
citation count." Will not tolerate medical facts being garbled or a trial being mis-described.

**Jobs-to-be-done:**
- An EB-1A read that maps **clinical-trial leadership → Leading/critical role**, **guideline
  authorship → Original contribution of major significance**, **NIH study-section + journal review →
  Judging**, and her **society fellowship → Membership** against the real expert-judged bar.
- Confirm she's on the **EB-1A 10-criteria pack** and get an honest read of which 3+ she clears — she
  suspects she's well over threshold but wants it argued *correctly*, not luckily.
- A draft that argues her trial/guideline/citations as sustained acclaim in medicine using *her* real
  facts — trial names, guideline, journals, society — with **no invented** trials, citations, or
  patient numbers.

**What good looks like:** the verdict and draft recognize that **guideline authorship and trial
leadership are her crown jewels** and map them to Original contribution and Leading/critical role —
not buried under a citation count; **Judging** captures her NIH study-section and manuscript review;
**Membership** is assessed against the "outstanding-achievement, judged-by-experts" standard. The
draft uses her real journals and trial designations and never fabricates a medical fact.

**Pet peeves:** her guideline reduced to "a publication" instead of original contribution of major
significance; a trial mis-described, a wrong phase, or an invented enrollment number; fabricated
citations or a society she doesn't belong to; the **EB-1A pack silently swapped for the O-1A 8-pack**
on default; "Artistic exhibitions: Met" nonsense for an oncologist; any line that drifts toward
giving *medical* or *legal* advice rather than drafting work product.

**Motivation (time-saved):** the LLM-less way is **$7.5k–$15k + 2–3 months** at a firm that
specializes in physician/researcher EB-1A self-petitions, where she'd still hand-assemble trial
documentation, the guideline, citation reports, and review records, then wait on a drafter who may
not understand oncology evidence. The app wins if it produces a correctly-mapped, citation-disciplined
first draft in an afternoon her own attorney can refine — saving the drafting weeks **and** the labor
of teaching a generalist why a guideline outranks a citation count. A draft that garbles a trial or
buries her contribution is worse than useless given her time is clinic time.

**Senior-quality bar:** at least as good as a senior physician-immigration attorney's draft — trial
leadership and guideline authorship argued as their strongest criteria, review service as judging,
fellowship as membership against the real bar, real medical facts only, no overclaiming. A senior
would reject any draft that fabricates a trial, citation, or society, mis-maps a guideline, shows the
wrong criteria pack, or strays into medical/legal advice.

**Scored acceptance criteria (applied identically every run):**
1. [ ] The qualify verdict reflects *her pasted medical record* (names her trials, guideline, journals, society, review service), not placeholders.
2. [ ] She is scored on the **correct EB-1A 10-criteria pack** with the ≥3 threshold — **not the O-1A 8-pack relabeled** by the default fallback.
3. [ ] **Guideline authorship/trial leadership** map to Original contribution and Leading/critical role; **study-section + journal review** map to Judging; **fellowship** to Membership against the expert-judged bar.
4. [ ] The draft cites only facts she supplied — **zero fabricated** trials, phases, enrollment numbers, citations, or societies; no nonsensical "Met" (e.g. Artistic exhibitions) for an oncologist.
5. [ ] The output stays *drafting work product* and never drifts into medical or legal advice; thin criteria are flagged honestly.
6. [ ] The not-legal-advice `DISCLAIMER` is present and she's clear her own attorney must review/sign.
7. [ ] Total time from paste → usable, correctly-mapped first draft is plausibly an afternoon, not the firm's 2–3 months + $7.5k–$15k.
