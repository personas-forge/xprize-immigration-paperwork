# Noa Grossman — the behind-the-scenes composer who must be read as a LEAD role without ever being on camera

**Background / lived experience:** Israeli-trained composer who writes scores for film, prestige TV,
and AAA video games, currently in the US on an O-1 nearing its end with two projects greenlit. His
record is strong but **invisible in the way arts criteria are usually illustrated** — he is never on
camera, never "starring," never the face of anything. He composed the **original score** for a
series that won a **guild award** and a feature that took a **festival music prize**; his game
soundtrack passed **40 million streams** on Spotify and the game itself sold millions; he's a member
of the **Society of Composers & Lyricists** and **ASCAP**; he's been **featured/interviewed** in a
trade outlet about his scoring process. His worry is structural: the O-1B criterion is literally
"**lead role in distinguished productions**," and every example a layperson (or a naive tool) reaches
for is an *actor* in a starring role. He needs the tool to understand that the **composer is a
principal creative lead** on a distinguished production even though he's behind the scenes — and to
map his streams/sales to **commercial/critical success**, his guild to **recognition from
organizations & experts**, his press to **reviews & press**. He has seen a colleague's O-1 stumble
because the petition described him as merely "contributing music" instead of holding a lead creative
role. A specialist firm quoted **$8k–$10k** and **8–10 weeks**.

**Voice:** thoughtful, precise about craft and credits, slightly anxious about being underread.
Talks in scores, cues, and credits — "I'm not the performer, I'm the composer; that *is* a lead
role." Forgives a plain UI; will not forgive being mapped as a minor contributor.

**Jobs-to-be-done:**
- Be screened as **O-1B arts** and have his **composer credit read as a lead/principal creative
  role** in distinguished productions — not downgraded to a generic "contributor."
- Map his streaming/sales numbers to **commercial/critical success**, guild membership to
  **recognition from organizations & experts**, and trade features to **reviews & press**.
- Organize the evidence (cue sheets, guild card, streaming dashboards, the trade feature) into the
  right O-1B buckets, get a draft, and **track his case** through Qualified → Evidence → Drafted →
  Review as he assembles it.

**What good looks like:** the screener treats "composed the original score for [distinguished
production]" as **lead role in distinguished productions**, not as nothing; the 40M streams and
multi-million game sales land under **record of major commercial/critical success**; the SCL/ASCAP
membership under **recognition from organizations & experts**; the verdict names *his* productions
and numbers; the roadmap shows his real stage so he always knows what's next.

**Pet peeves:** the tool only "getting" lead roles for on-camera/starring talent and leaving a
composer's lead-role criterion empty; his streams mis-bucketed as "press" instead of commercial
success; a fabricated stream count, sales figure, or award; being described as "providing music"
instead of holding a lead creative role; a roadmap that doesn't reflect what he actually did.

**Motivation (time-saved):** the LLM-less way is **$8k–$10k + 8–10 weeks** of a firm — and the real
risk is a petition that *underframes* a behind-the-scenes artist and draws an RFE on "lead role."
The app wins if it correctly reads composer-as-lead, maps his numbers to the right criteria, and
drafts in an afternoon. If it can only recognize on-camera leads, it leaves his strongest criterion
blank and saves him nothing — he'd have to re-argue the whole thing.

**Senior-quality bar:** at least as good as an arts-specialist associate who knows a film/TV/game
**composer holds a principal creative role** in a distinguished production, and who maps streaming
and sales to commercial/critical success and guild membership to expert/organizational recognition.
A senior would reject a draft that leaves "lead role" empty for a composer, mis-buckets his numbers,
or invents a single stream/sale/award.

**Scored acceptance criteria (applied identically every run):**
1. [ ] He is screened against the **O-1B arts six**, and his **composer credit is read as a lead/principal creative role in distinguished productions** — the lead-role criterion is engaged for a behind-the-scenes artist, not left empty because he isn't "starring."
2. [ ] The pack is genuinely the **O-1B six** (lead role / recognition / reviews & press / commercial-critical success / org-expert recognition / high salary), **not** the O-1A eight relabeled (no "scholarly articles"/"patent" on his screen).
3. [ ] His streaming/sales (40M+ streams, multi-million game sales) map to **record of major commercial/critical success**; SCL/ASCAP to **recognition from organizations & experts**; the trade feature to **reviews & press** — each named, each in the right place.
4. [ ] The verdict and draft cite only facts he supplied — **zero fabricated** streams, sales, awards, credits, or guilds.
5. [ ] In the Evidence Vault his documents land in the correct O-1B buckets (or Unsorted), exhibit numbers are monotonic, and the coverage read is honest; extracted facts match the documents.
6. [ ] The case roadmap/stepper reflects his **real** stage (Qualified → Evidence → Drafted → Review …) and always shows an obvious next action; the `DISCLAIMER` rides on every AI payload and an attorney must review/sign.
7. [ ] Total time from paste → correctly-framed verdict + organized evidence + usable draft is plausibly an afternoon, beating the firm's weeks.
