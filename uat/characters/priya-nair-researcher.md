# Dr. Priya Nair — the rigor-first researcher who will fact-check the machine

**Background / lived experience:** Postdoc-turned-staff-scientist at a US research institute, on
an expiring J-1 with a looming clock. 30+ peer-reviewed papers, an h-index she can recite,
citations in the low thousands, two best-paper awards, peer-review and grant-panel service.
She priced a traditional firm at **$5k–$8k** (some quoted $10k+) and balked — not only the money
but the **2–3 months** and the 500–700-page packet she'd still have to feed them. She has watched
labmates get O-1 **RFEs** over sloppy "extraordinary ability" framing and weak comparable-evidence
arguments, so she is allergic to anything that *sounds* impressive but is legally hollow. She has
tried ChatGPT for a first draft and found it confidently wrong about the criteria.

**Voice:** precise, citation-minded, slightly wary. Talks in evidence and counter-arguments —
"says who, by what standard?" Will forgive plainness; will not forgive a confident error.

**Jobs-to-be-done:**
- Find out *honestly* whether her record clears O-1A (and whether EB-1A is the smarter long game).
- Get a petition letter that maps **her actual** publications/citations/awards to the right
  criteria — original-contributions, scholarly articles, judging, awards — with defensible framing.
- Know exactly which criteria are thin so she can shore them up before her attorney sees it.

**What good looks like:** the verdict names *her* evidence, not generic placeholders; the criterion
crosswalk is the one a real adjudicator uses (8 CFR §214.2(o)(3)(iii)); the draft reads like a
careful immigration drafter wrote it, with no invented facts and no overclaiming.

**Pet peeves:** invented citations or awards; "extraordinary" asserted without evidence; the eight
criteria summarized wrong; green/"met" badges on criteria she didn't actually satisfy; anything
that reads like a horoscope ("you seem accomplished!").

**Motivation (time-saved):** the LLM-less way is **2–3 months + $5–8k** (engage a firm, assemble
700 pages, draft letters). She'll adopt the app if it produces a *trustworthy* first draft in an
afternoon that her own attorney can refine — i.e. collapses the drafting weeks to hours **without**
lowering the bar. A fast draft she has to throw away saves nothing.

**Senior-quality bar:** at least as good as the draft a strong immigration paralegal would produce
for her after reading her CV — accurate criteria, her real evidence cited, honest about gaps. A
senior would reject anything that fabricates, miscategorizes evidence, or asserts a criterion is
met without the supporting fact.

**Scored acceptance criteria (applied identically every run):**
1. [ ] The qualify verdict reflects *her pasted record* (names her real evidence), not a template.
2. [ ] The eight O-1A criteria and the ≥3 threshold are represented correctly; nothing she didn't claim shows as "Met".
3. [ ] The draft cites only facts she supplied — **zero fabricated** awards/citations/publications.
4. [ ] Each drafted section crosswalks to a specific, correct criterion (original contribution, scholarly articles, judging, awards…).
5. [ ] Thin/weak criteria are flagged honestly as gaps, not papered over.
6. [ ] The not-legal-advice `DISCLAIMER` is present and she's clear an attorney must review/sign.
7. [ ] Total time from paste → usable first draft is plausibly an afternoon, not days.
