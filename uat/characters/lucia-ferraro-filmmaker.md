# Lucia Ferraro — the documentary director who must be scored on the O-1B SIX, not the O-1A eight

**Background / lived experience:** Italian-born documentary director, ten years into a working
career, currently in the US on an O-1 that's expiring with a new film in post and a US distributor
waiting. Her record is unmistakably **arts**, not business: her last feature was a **Sundance**
official selection and screened at **IDFA** and **Hot Docs**; it drew a **New York Times** Critic's
Pick and a **Variety** review; she signed a **theatrical/streaming distribution deal**; she's a
voting member of the **Directors Guild** and the **International Documentary Association**, and she
has **directed** (lead creative role) every film she's made. She has been burned before: a prior
attorney's paralegal ran her through an O-1A-shaped intake and asked her for "scholarly articles"
and "patents" — criteria that have *nothing* to do with a filmmaker — and the resulting RFE asked
why she'd submitted irrelevant evidence. So her single biggest fear with any tool is that it will
screen her against the wrong pack: the **O-1A eight** relabeled, instead of the **O-1B arts six**
(lead role in distinguished productions, national/international recognition, reviews & press, record
of major commercial/critical success, recognition from organizations & experts, high salary). A
firm quoted her **$8k–$12k** and **two to three months**; she'd rather spend an afternoon if the
tool actually understands what a director's evidence is.

**Voice:** warm but exacting, story-first, allergic to box-checking that misreads her craft. Talks
in films, festivals, and reviews — "that's not how this field is judged." Forgives plainness;
will not forgive being asked for a patent.

**Jobs-to-be-done:**
- Confirm she's screened as **O-1B arts** and scored against the **right six criteria** — and catch
  it instantly if she's been handed the O-1A eight in disguise.
- Get her Sundance/IDFA selections, NYT/Variety reviews, distribution deal, and guild membership
  mapped onto **lead role / recognition / reviews & press / commercial-critical success / org-expert
  recognition** correctly.
- Organize her evidence (selections, reviews, the deal memo, guild card) into the right buckets with
  an exhibit index, and get a petition draft her attorney can red-line.

**What good looks like:** the criteria she's scored on read *lead role in distinguished
productions*, *reviews & press*, *record of major commercial/critical success* — the **O-1B six** —
not "scholarly articles" and "patents"; the verdict names *her* films, *her* festivals, *her*
reviews; the evidence vault files the NYT review under reviews & press (not "judging"), the Sundance
selection under recognition, the distribution deal under commercial/critical success; the draft
argues distinction the way a real arts-O-1 petition does.

**Pet peeves:** being shown the **O-1A eight** when she's an arts case; "scholarly articles" or
"patent" criteria on a director's screen; her Variety review mis-bucketed; a fabricated festival,
review quote, or box-office number; the tool treating "director" as a business critical-role rather
than the *lead creative role in distinguished productions* it actually is; any green "Met" she
can't evidence.

**Motivation (time-saved):** the LLM-less way is **$8k–$12k + 2–3 months** of a firm (arts O-1
firms quote differently than O-1A business firms, and the good ones who *get* documentary are
pricey) plus the risk of an RFE if the packet argues the wrong criteria. The app wins if it screens
her correctly as O-1B, organizes her real evidence into the right six buckets, and drafts a
distinction argument **in an afternoon**. If it scores her against the O-1A eight, it's not faster —
it's a trap that hands her the exact RFE she's already lived through.

**Senior-quality bar:** at least as good as an arts-specialist immigration associate who knows a
documentary director is judged on **lead creative role, critical reviews, festival recognition, and
commercial/critical success** — and would never ask a filmmaker for a patent. A senior would reject
any draft built on the O-1A eight, any mis-bucketed review, or any invented festival/review/deal.
