# Oluwaseun "Seun" Adeyemi — the founder who wants the green card, not another visa renewal

**Background / lived experience:** Founder/CEO of a Series-A fintech (~50 staff, payments
infrastructure for African diaspora remittances) operating in the US, currently on an O-1A he
self-funded two years ago. He is **done renewing visas** — he wants permanent residency so he can
raise, hire, and not have his immigration status be a diligence item in every term sheet. His record
is **founder-shaped and strong**: **Forbes 30 Under 30 (Finance)**, regulatory + press coverage
(TechCrunch, a Bloomberg quote, a CFPB-adjacent regulatory mention), **$14M ARR** and a published
Series-A round, a **board seat** at a fintech-for-good nonprofit, and two industry panels. He is
deliberately pursuing **EB-1A self-petition** — the green card — not just rolling his O-1A. He is
sharp enough to know EB-1A is a *higher* bar (sustained acclaim, final-merits "small percentage at
the top") than O-1A, and he wants the tool to tell him honestly whether he clears it or should bridge
through O-1A first. He is **distinct from Sam** (an early-OPT founder chasing a first O-1A); Seun is
later-stage, has real revenue and press, and is buying for permanence.

**Voice:** decisive, ROI-framed, board-room calm. Asks "what's the strongest path and the real
probability, not the optimistic one?" Treats the verdict like a diligence memo — he will forward it
to counsel and his board, so it cannot overclaim or it embarrasses him.

**Jobs-to-be-done:**
- A best-path read that reasons **O-1A vs EB-1A** for *his* facts — and is honest that EB-1A is the
  harder green-card bar, not a coin-flip recommendation.
- Confirm the tool shows him the **correct EB-1A 10-criteria pack** (Awards, Membership, Press,
  Judging, Original contribution, Scholarly articles, Artistic exhibitions, Leading/critical role,
  High remuneration, Commercial success in the arts) — and that it isn't silently the O-1A 8-pack
  relabeled because the classification defaulted.
- A draft that maps Forbes/press → Press, board + CEO role → Leading/critical role, revenue →
  Commercial success / High remuneration — using his real numbers — and a cold-eyed credibility read
  of the product before he spends a dollar or his board's attention.

**What good looks like:** the funnel recommends a path with *reasoning* tied to his evidence and is
candid about EB-1A's final-merits bar; the criteria screen shows the **EB-1A pack with its
arts-flavored criteria visible** (Artistic exhibitions, Commercial success in the arts) so he can
tell it's the *real* EB-1A pack and reason about which apply to a fintech founder — not O-1A's 8
relabeled. The draft cites his real ARR, round, and Forbes year, names the right criteria, and reads
like something he could attach to a board update without cringing.

**Pet peeves:** being shown **8 criteria** and "EB-1A" in the same breath (the O-1A fallback leaking
through — an instant trust-killer for someone who'll check); a best-path that just says "EB-1A!"
with no reasoning about the higher bar; a fabricated ARR, round size, or Forbes year; "Artistic
exhibitions: Met" hallucinated for a payments company; positioning that implies the tool files or
advises rather than drafts for *his* attorney.

**Motivation (time-saved):** the LLM-less way for an EB-1A self-petition is **$7.5k–$15k + 2–3
months** of firm drafting (self-petitions cost *more* because he carries the full burden of proof,
with no employer), then 6–12 months at USCIS. The app wins if it gives him a defensible path
recommendation and a correctly-packed first draft in an afternoon — saving the drafting spend and,
more importantly, **not steering him wrong** on O-1A-vs-EB-1A, which is a decision worth far more
than the fee. A confidently-wrong best-path or an O-1A-pack-mislabeled-as-EB-1A actively costs him.

**Senior-quality bar:** at least as good as a senior business-immigration attorney's intake memo —
an honest O-1A-vs-EB-1A path call with reasoning, the correct EB-1A criteria pack, founder evidence
mapped to the right criteria, real numbers only, no overclaiming on "sustained acclaim." A senior
would reject any draft that fabricates revenue/round/Forbes facts, shows the wrong criteria pack, or
soft-pedals how high the EB-1A final-merits bar actually is.

**Scored acceptance criteria (applied identically every run):**
1. [ ] The best-path comparison reasons **O-1A vs EB-1A** for *his* facts and is honest that EB-1A is the higher green-card bar — not a coin-flip.
2. [ ] The criteria shown for his EB-1A case are the **correct EB-1A 10-criteria pack** (Artistic exhibitions + Commercial-success-in-the-arts present), **not the O-1A 8-pack relabeled** by the default fallback.
3. [ ] The verdict/draft reflects *his pasted record* (Forbes year, ARR, round, board seat) — Press, Leading/critical role, Commercial success, High remuneration mapped correctly.
4. [ ] The draft cites only facts he supplied — **zero fabricated** revenue, round size, Forbes year, or board roles; no "Met" on criteria a fintech founder didn't claim.
5. [ ] Positioning is unmistakable across `/` `/pricing` and the case: a **drafting tool, not a law firm**; *his* attorney reviews/signs; **not legal advice**; the `DISCLAIMER` rides on AI output.
6. [ ] The shareable verdict reads as a credible diligence memo (no overclaim/cheese) he'd forward to his board and counsel, leaking nothing private he didn't choose.
7. [ ] Total time from paste → usable, correctly-packed EB-1A first draft is plausibly an afternoon, not the firm's 2–3 months + $7.5k–$15k.
