# Ravi Menon — the postdoc racing an expiring J-1 toward a faculty offer

**Background / lived experience:** Condensed-matter physics postdoc at a US national-lab-affiliated
group, on a **J-1 that expires in under a year** with a two-year home-residency requirement he's
hoping to navigate around — the clock is the whole story. He's chasing tenure-track faculty offers,
and a green card he holds *himself* (EB-1A self-petition) would make him far more hireable than a
candidate the department has to sponsor. His record is **classically academic and genuinely strong**:
**h-index ~22**, citations in the low thousands, first/senior authorship in Phys. Rev. Letters and
Nature Physics, **peer review for two journals**, a couple of **competitive early-career grants/
fellowships**, invited conference talks, and a society membership that's selective-ish but he's not
sure it clears the EB-1A "judged by recognized experts" membership bar. He is pursuing **EB-1A**, not
O-1A — he needs the green card, not another temporary status — but he is honest enough to want the
tool to tell him if his case is borderline on the EB-1A final-merits standard and whether he should
have a backup. He is **distinct from Priya** (who is weighing O-1A first and is staff-scientist, not
faculty-track) and from the industry scientists — Ravi is pure academia, grant-and-journal shaped,
under acute time pressure.

**Voice:** careful, self-critical, a little anxious about the clock. Speaks in the field's own
metrics — "h-index, impact factor, which journals count" — and is the first to flag his own weak
spots. Wants the honest gap read more than the flattering one because he doesn't have time to file a
loser.

**Jobs-to-be-done:**
- An honest EB-1A read on *academic* evidence: **Scholarly articles** and **Judging** (his peer
  review) are his strongest; he needs **Membership** assessed against the real "outstanding-
  achievement, judged-by-experts" bar, not waved through.
- Confirm he's being scored on the **EB-1A 10-criteria pack** (with Awards, Membership, Press,
  Judging, Original contribution, Scholarly articles, etc.) and an honest read of which 3+ he clears
  and which are thin — fast, because of the J-1 clock.
- A draft mapping papers → Scholarly articles, peer review → Judging, grants/fellowships → Awards,
  novel results → Original contribution, with citations to his real venues and an honest gap note.

**What good looks like:** the verdict names *his* journals, *his* h-index/citation count, *his*
grants and peer-review service; **Membership** is assessed skeptically (does his society actually
require outstanding achievement judged by experts, or is it dues-paying?) rather than auto-"Met";
the draft argues sustained acclaim from his real publication record without inflating his h-index or
claiming press he doesn't have. It tells him plainly which criteria are gaps under the EB-1A bar.

**Pet peeves:** a dues-only membership rubber-stamped as "Met"; an inflated h-index or citation
count; press/awards he doesn't have invented to pad the count; the **EB-1A pack silently replaced by
the O-1A 8-pack** because the classification defaulted; cheerful "you qualify!" framing that ignores
the EB-1A final-merits standard; a draft that reads like generic AI filler instead of a physicist's
careful prose.

**Motivation (time-saved):** the LLM-less way is **$7.5k–$15k + 2–3 months** at a firm (academic
EB-1A self-petitions, full burden of proof on him), then 6–12 months at USCIS — time he may not have
on a J-1. The app wins if it gives him a fast, honest go/no-go and a correctly-mapped first draft in
an afternoon he can hand to counsel, **and** flags his thin criteria so he doesn't waste the clock
filing a case that draws an RFE or denial. A flattering-but-wrong read is the worst outcome — it
costs him the months he doesn't have.

**Senior-quality bar:** at least as good as a senior researcher-immigration attorney's read — an
honest EB-1A assessment that doesn't over-credit a soft membership, maps academic evidence to the
right criteria, cites real venues/metrics only, and is candid about the final-merits bar and his
gaps. A senior would reject anything that inflates his metrics, invents press/awards, rubber-stamps
his membership, or shows him the wrong criteria pack.

**Scored acceptance criteria (applied identically every run):**
1. [ ] The qualify verdict reflects *his pasted academic record* (names his journals, h-index/citations, grants, peer review), not placeholders.
2. [ ] He is scored on the **correct EB-1A 10-criteria pack** with the ≥3 threshold — **not the O-1A 8-pack relabeled** by the default fallback.
3. [ ] **Membership** is assessed against the real "outstanding-achievement, judged-by-experts" bar, not auto-"Met"; **Scholarly articles** and **Judging** map to his papers and peer review.
4. [ ] The draft cites only facts he supplied — **zero fabricated** h-index, citations, venues, grants, awards, or press.
5. [ ] Thin criteria (e.g. a soft membership, no press) are flagged honestly as gaps under the EB-1A bar, not papered over.
6. [ ] The not-legal-advice `DISCLAIMER` is present and he's clear his own attorney must review/sign.
7. [ ] Total time from paste → usable, correctly-packed first draft is plausibly an afternoon, not the firm's 2–3 months — fast enough to matter against the J-1 clock.
