# Kenji Watanabe — the maintainer whose résumé is a commit history, not a CV

**Background / lived experience:** Staff infrastructure engineer at a mid-size US scale-up, in the
US on an H-1B he's tired of being lottery-hostage to. His real life's work is a CNCF-adjacent
open-source project he co-maintains — **~38k GitHub stars**, a Helm chart pulled millions of times,
and a dependency graph that runs inside named companies (he can point to Datadog, Shopify, and two
banks using it in production). He has given a **KubeCon keynote** and two conference talks, reviews
PRs and RFCs as a core maintainer, and triages CVEs for the project. What he does **not** have is a
single peer-reviewed academic paper, a PhD, or a "Best Paper" award — his entire evidentiary record
is OSS-shaped: stars, downloads, adoption, maintainer status, conference stages, and a GitHub
contribution graph. Every immigration calculator he's tried, and ChatGPT, kept trying to slot him
into the *researcher* mold ("list your publications and citations") and then concluded he was weak —
because they couldn't read OSS impact as evidence. He priced two firms at **$8k–$12k** and a 6–10
week drafting cycle, and both wanted him to hand-write a "contributions narrative" first anyway.

**Voice:** terse, technical, allergic to hand-waving. Talks in metrics and provenance — "stars
aren't a vanity number, they're third-party adoption; here's the dependency graph." Will respect a
tool that maps the *right* USCIS criterion to the *right* artifact; will close the tab the instant
it asks him for citation counts he doesn't have or calls his project a "publication."

**Jobs-to-be-done:**
- Get an honest O-1A read that scores OSS evidence on its own terms — adoption, maintainer status,
  keynote — not a researcher rubric that penalizes him for having no papers.
- See **Original contribution** and **Critical role** correctly mapped from his project (the library
  itself, the maintainer seat, the named adopters), and **Judging** mapped from his PR/RFC review.
- Draft a petition letter that argues his GitHub metrics as comparable evidence the way a strong
  immigration drafter would — and flags honestly that **Scholarly articles** is his thin criterion.

**What good looks like:** the verdict names *his* project, *his* star/download numbers, *his*
keynote — not "your publications." Original-contribution and Critical-role sections cite the
adoption/maintainer facts he pasted; Judging is anchored on peer review of code/RFCs, not academic
refereeing. The draft argues OSS impact as **comparable evidence** under 8 CFR §214.2(o)(3)(iii)
without pretending he has papers he doesn't.

**Pet peeves:** any output that treats absence of academic papers as disqualifying; "Scholarly
articles: Met" hallucinated from his conference *talks*; invented star counts, fake company names,
or a download number he didn't supply; a Critical-role section that names an employer he never
worked at; the eight criteria summarized as if he were a postdoc.

**Motivation (time-saved):** the LLM-less way is **6–10 weeks + $8k–$12k** — engage a firm, then
still hand-write the contributions narrative and assemble a 400+ page exhibit packet himself. The
app wins only if it turns his pasted OSS record into a *trustworthy, correctly-mapped* first draft
in an afternoon that his own attorney can red-line — collapsing the drafting weeks to hours without
mis-reading code impact as research. A draft that buckets his work wrong saves him nothing; he'd
spend longer un-doing it than writing from scratch.

**Senior-quality bar:** at least as good as a senior immigration paralegal who *gets* tech would
produce after reading his GitHub — OSS adoption argued as comparable evidence, maintainer status as
critical role, code review as judging, and an honest "papers are your gap" note. A senior would
reject anything that fabricates metrics, miscategorizes a talk as a publication, or quietly defaults
him into a researcher template.

**Scored acceptance criteria (applied identically every run):**
1. [ ] The qualify verdict reflects *his pasted OSS record* (names his real project, stars/downloads, keynote), not a publications template.
2. [ ] The **eight O-1A criteria** (Awards, Membership, Press, Judging, Original contribution, Scholarly articles, Critical role, High remuneration) and the ≥3 threshold are represented correctly; nothing he didn't supply shows as "Met".
3. [ ] **Original contribution** maps to his project/adoption and **Critical role** to his maintainer seat / named adopters — not to a paper or a job he never held.
4. [ ] The draft cites only facts he supplied — **zero fabricated** stars, downloads, company names, or talks.
5. [ ] **Scholarly articles** is flagged honestly as his thin criterion (talks ≠ papers), not papered over as "Met".
6. [ ] The not-legal-advice `DISCLAIMER` is present and he's clear his own attorney must review/sign.
7. [ ] Total time from paste → usable, correctly-mapped first draft is plausibly an afternoon, not the firm's 6–10 weeks.
