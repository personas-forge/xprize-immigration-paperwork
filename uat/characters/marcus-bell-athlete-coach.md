# Marcus Bell — the elite climber whose case is real but PAPERLESS, and needs an honest "scholarly is N/A" read

**Background / lived experience:** British professional sport climber and **national-team coach**,
moving to the US to compete and coach at a high-performance center; he qualifies under **O-1A
athletics** (extraordinary ability in athletics — same eight criteria as sciences/business, applied
to sport). His record is genuinely elite but **athlete-shaped, not paper-shaped**: multiple **IFSC
World Cup podiums**, two **national championship** titles, a **selective national-team membership**,
a **critical coaching role** that took a junior squad to international medals, and **sponsorship
deals** (a major apparel brand + a hardware sponsor) that are his "high remuneration." What he does
**not** have is the thing the O-1A criteria assume for scientists: **scholarly articles** (he's an
athlete, not an academic — zero papers), and his **press** is thin and mostly climbing-media niche
rather than mainstream. His fear is the inverse of overclaiming: he's seen tools and even a lawyer
try to **force a fit** — inventing a "publication" out of a magazine interview, or padding "judging"
from a single local comp — and that kind of stretch is exactly what draws an RFE. He wants the tool
to be **honest**: map awards / membership / critical role / remuneration where he's genuinely strong,
and plainly say **scholarly articles is thin/not applicable** for an athlete rather than fabricating
a fit. A sports-specialist firm quoted **$6k–$9k** and **6–8 weeks** (athletics O-1A is priced
differently than the arts O-1B).

**Voice:** direct, competitive, low-BS — a coach's bluntness. Talks in results and rankings — "I've
got podiums and a team that wins; I don't have papers, so don't pretend I do." Respects honesty
above flattery; instantly distrusts a tool that inflates him.

**Jobs-to-be-done:**
- Be screened as **O-1A athletics** with his **World Cup podiums / national titles → awards**,
  **national-team → membership**, **coaching the squad to medals → critical role**, **sponsorships →
  high remuneration** mapped correctly and *strongly*.
- Get an **honest** read that **scholarly articles is thin / not applicable** and **press is weak** —
  flagged as gaps, **not** force-fitted from an interview or a local comp.
- Get a draft that argues the criteria he genuinely meets (athletes need ≥3 of 8; he clears several)
  without padding, and a share link for his federation/sponsor.

**What good looks like:** the verdict scores him **strong** on awards, membership, critical role, and
remuneration; marks **scholarly articles "None"** (neutral, not green, not invented) with an honest
note that papers aren't expected for an athlete; flags **press as thin**; names *his* podiums,
titles, team results, and sponsors; the draft argues the met criteria cleanly and doesn't manufacture
a publication or a judging role he doesn't have.

**Pet peeves:** a tool inventing a "publication" from a magazine interview to fill scholarly
articles; "judging" padded from one local youth comp; any criterion shown green that he can't
defend; a forced fit that would invite an RFE; flattery ("you seem accomplished!") instead of a
clear-eyed read of which of the eight he actually clears; a fabricated podium, title, or sponsor
figure.

**Motivation (time-saved):** the LLM-less way is **$6k–$9k + 6–8 weeks** with a sports-immigration
firm. The app wins if it maps his athletic evidence to the right O-1A criteria, is **honest about
the thin ones**, and drafts in an afternoon. A tool that pads scholarly/press to look complete
doesn't save him time — it builds the exact RFE-bait packet he's trying to avoid, and he'll go back
to the firm that knows athletes.

**Senior-quality bar:** at least as good as a sports-immigration associate who knows an athlete
clears O-1A on **awards + membership + critical role + remuneration**, and who would **never**
fabricate a scholarly article or stretch judging to force a fit — flagging the inapplicable criteria
honestly instead. A senior would reject any draft that invents a paper, pads a criterion, or shows a
green "Met" the evidence can't support.

**Scored acceptance criteria (applied identically every run):**
1. [ ] He is screened as **O-1A athletics** against the **eight** criteria, and his athletic evidence maps correctly: World Cup podiums + national titles → **Awards**; national-team → **Membership**; coaching the squad to medals → **Critical role**; sponsorships → **High remuneration**.
2. [ ] **Scholarly articles** is read **honestly as thin / not applicable** for an athlete — scored "None" (neutral, never green), **NOT** fabricated from an interview or padded; **press** is flagged as a genuine gap, not inflated.
3. [ ] No criterion shows green "Met" that his evidence can't support; the ≥3-of-8 threshold is represented correctly and he can see he clears it on the strong criteria alone.
4. [ ] The verdict names *his* real evidence (named podiums, national titles, team, sponsors) — not placeholders — and the overall read is clear-eyed, not flattering filler.
5. [ ] The draft cites only facts he supplied — **zero fabricated** podiums, titles, papers, judging roles, or sponsor figures — and argues only the criteria he genuinely meets.
6. [ ] The `DISCLAIMER` rides on every AI payload and he's clear an attorney must review/sign; the tool screens but never *forces* a fit to look more complete.
7. [ ] Time from paste → honest, correctly-mapped verdict + un-padded draft is plausibly an afternoon, beating the sports-firm weeks; the share link reads as a credible athletics verdict for his federation/sponsor.
