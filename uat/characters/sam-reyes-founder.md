# Sam Reyes — the time-poor founder who'll DIY if the tool is genuinely good

**Background / lived experience:** Solo-ish technical founder, on an OPT clock about to lapse,
raising a seed round and shipping product — immigration is the thing they keep deprioritizing
until it's an emergency. Evidence is *founder-shaped*: press (TechCrunch), GitHub with real stars,
a YC batch, investor letters, revenue, judging a hackathon. They've heard O-1A is "the founder
visa" and that a firm wants **$8k–$15k** and a few months. Burned once by a generic legal
template service that produced a packet a friend's lawyer called "a liability". Lives in
Linear/Notion; expects software to be fast, clear, and not make them read a treatise.

**Voice:** fast, informal, outcome-driven. "Just tell me: do I qualify, what's it cost, how fast,
what do you need from me." Allergic to ceremony and legalese; loves a crisp verdict and a share link.

**Jobs-to-be-done:**
- Get a believable yes/no/maybe **in minutes**, and know which visa (O-1A vs O-1B vs EB-1A) is the
  fastest realistic path.
- Turn their messy founder evidence into a real petition letter draft without learning immigration law.
- Decide DIY-via-tool vs pay-a-firm — and if the tool is good, just go.

**What good looks like:** the screener instantly turns "founder noise" (press, GitHub, funding)
into the right criteria (press coverage, original contributions, high remuneration, critical role);
the draft sounds like a real petition, not ChatGPT; pricing is honest and self-serve.

**Pet peeves:** walls of legalese; being told "consult an attorney" with no actual output; slow
spinners with no progress; a verdict so hedged it's useless; surprise costs; anything that feels
like a lead-gen funnel rather than a tool that does the work.

**Motivation (time-saved):** LLM-less way = **$8k–$15k + 2–3 months** of firm back-and-forth, or
worse, doing nothing until the clock forces a panic. The app wins if it gives a real draft
**today** for a fraction of the cost. If generating is *slower* than they'd expect (waiting minutes
with no feedback) or the output needs a full rewrite, they bounce back to "I'll just hire someone".

**Senior-quality bar:** good enough that their *own* attorney looks at it and says "this is a solid
starting draft" rather than "start over". Founder-evidence must be mapped to criteria correctly
(press → published material about them; GitHub/product → original contributions of major
significance; round/ARR → high remuneration), with no invented metrics.

**Scored acceptance criteria (applied identically every run):**
1. [ ] Verdict in well under ~5 min, with a clear yes/no/maybe and the strongest visa path called out.
2. [ ] Founder evidence is mapped to the *correct* criteria (press, original contribution, critical role, high remuneration).
3. [ ] "Best path" comparison across O-1A/O-1B/EB-1A is legible and reasoned, not a coin flip.
4. [ ] The draft uses their real supplied facts; **no fabricated** press, stars, ARR, or titles.
5. [ ] Pricing/tokens are clear and self-serve — they can see what a draft costs before committing.
6. [ ] During generation there's visible progress; no dead spinner that makes them think it hung.
7. [ ] The share/verdict link is something they'd actually send to a cofounder or post — credible, not cheesy.
